FEATURED EFFORT: PALMS UNHOUSED MUTUAL AID

Palms Unhoused Mutual Aid (PUMA) is a localized care network which aims to meet community needs among unhoused and housing insecure neighbors across Mid-City and Palms. PUMA tackles this goal through a variety of efforts and coalitions, including weekly outreach with food, water, hygiene, and survival items to encampments and unhoused neighbors, collaboration with LA Neighbors 4 Neighbors‚Äô monthly Resource Center, weekly harm reduction at local encampments, collaboration with Palms Action Collective to attain grants for and distribute hygiene kits with direct cash assistance, local advocacy on issues of houselessness, and much more.

PUMA‚Äôs mutual aid efforts are collaborative between housed and unhoused neighbors. In early February, PUMA launched a headlamp drive for our unhoused neighbors at the suggestion of members of the community in order to offer a reliable source of light through the night. Regarding advocacy, over the past months local city offices and service providers have come to learn about what PUMA does and have invited PUMA members to participate in meetings dealing with issues of houselessness, which offers the opportunity for PUMA to give perspectives as a group that is consistently doing on-the-ground work in collaboration with unhoused neighbors.

PUMA strongly urges city officials to further engage with and call unhoused neighbors to be part of these problem-solving meetings.
